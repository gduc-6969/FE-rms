Improvement: Expand to a “Flexible Session-Based Reservation” Model
Instead of limiting reservations to a single fixed time window, you can design a system that supports multiple service sessions per day or allows customers to book at any time while limiting the number of reservations per short time slot (e.g., every 15–30 minutes).

Define Service Sessions
Identify the restaurant’s operating hours and divide them into clear service sessions, for example:

Lunch Session: 10:00 – 14:00
Dinner Session: 17:00 – 22:00

Within each session, enable time-slot reservations — for instance, allowing bookings every 30 minutes.

Benefits:
Customers can choose an arrival time that best suits their schedule, while the restaurant can still effectively control capacity by setting a maximum number of tables per time slot.
Reservation Window (Advance Booking Deadline)
You can retain the core idea of having a limited booking window, but make it more flexible. For example:
For the Dinner Session (17:00–22:00), customers can make reservations from 08:00 to 16:00 on the same day. After 16:00, the system stops accepting new reservations for that evening’s session.
During the actual service session, the restaurant can still accept walk-in customers if tables are available, while giving priority to customers who booked in advance.
Advantages of this approach:

Maximizes utilization of tables by accepting walk-ins after the booking cutoff.
Avoids turning away potential spontaneous customers.
Ensures staff have sufficient preparation time, as the final number of reservations is known by 16:00.
